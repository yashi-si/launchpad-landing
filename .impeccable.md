## Design Context

### Users
Marketing teams and non-technical professionals who need to build landing pages, campaign sites, and microsites quickly. They value speed and simplicity over technical control. They're busy, often juggling multiple campaigns, and want tools that feel intuitive and get out of their way.

### Brand Personality
**Friendly, approachable, empowering.** Launchpad should feel like a capable teammate, not intimidating software. The tone is confident but warm -- like a smart friend who makes complex things simple.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Visual tone**: Bold & modern with a friendly edge. Strong colors, gradients, and contrast -- but rounded corners, generous spacing, and approachable typography keep it human.
- **References**: Notion (clarity, whitespace), Canva (approachable, colorful), Framer (bold, modern)
- **Anti-references**: Enterprise software (cold, dense), overly minimal sites (sterile, no personality)
- **Theme**: Light mode only
- **Primary color**: Blue (#2563eb) -- extend with vibrant supporting palette
- **Typography**: System font stack currently; open to a friendlier web font

### Design Principles
1. **Clarity first** -- Every element should earn its place. If it doesn't help the user understand or act, remove it.
2. **Confidence through simplicity** -- Bold visuals and strong hierarchy make the product feel powerful without being complex.
3. **Warmth at scale** -- Use color, rounded shapes, and human language to keep the experience approachable even as features grow.
4. **Speed is a feature** -- The design should feel fast: quick to scan, quick to understand, quick to act on.
5. **Accessible by default** -- Meet WCAG AA standards. Good contrast, clear focus states, readable type sizes.
